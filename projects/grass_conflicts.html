Grass Conflicts

Watch the full video here

Grass Conflicts is a minigame based on Turf Wars from Mineplex.
It's a team based game, where the objective is to take over the entire enemy team's turf.

Your team gets 1 line of turf when an enemy dies, and as the game goes on, this number grows higher and higher.
You may only place and break blocks on your side of the turf.
Similarly to Turf Wars, you can build on your team's side during peace (Grass) phase, and fight only during the fighting (Conflicts) phase.

The kill streak death animation

As time goes on, air drops will start spawning, which contain useful & chaotic loot inside of them.

Airdrops & the various items of Grass Conflicts

If you're wondering why it's called Grass Conflicts, it's an inside joke between my friends. I googled synonyms for the words "turf" and "wars", and picked the first two words that came up. And thus, Grass Conflicts was born.

Coding Process

I challenged myself to create this project in under a week, and it has helped me a lot in understanding how to write minigame plugins, and how to work with Kotlin.
There are a lot of things I'd have done differently in hindsight, and lessons I've learned that I've taken with me to my next projects.

I used the FAWE API to handle large block update operations such as: Removing blocks, moving the red line and resetting the map.

For storing data in items (such as uses left), I used Paper's Persistent Data Container (PDC).
